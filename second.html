How the School says about themself.

Known locally as "The Rural" and located in Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island, Canada,
we are home to approximately 1000 students, 70 staff, and the Rural Raiders teams.
The modern design of our school with its unusual angles and curves symbolizes the waves of the sea that surround
our beautiful Island.Committed to excellence in all aspects of education; CRHS strives to create a welcoming, safe,
and caring environment where all students have the opportunity to reach their potential as lifelong
learners and as respectful members of a global society.

How Wiki says about the school

Charlottetown Rural High School (CRHS), known colloquially as "The Rural",
is a Canadian secondary school in Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island.
Students that attend the school come from the north and east parts of Queens County,
including the City of Charlottetown and the town of Stratford. The school is administratively part of the Public Schools Branch on Prince Edward Island.
Its official colours are red and white and the mascot is an osprey, referred to also as a Raider.
The sports teams from "The Rural" are called the Rural Raiders.

History and characteristics

In 1966, Charlottetown Rural High School was built in the growing suburban community of West Royalty,
north of the municipal limits of the city of Charlottetown.In 1994, The school was completely renovated to its present configuration
which saw a new cafeteria and specialist instruction space added. The renovations saw the building's exterior and interior remodelled using
a modern design of unusual angles and curves that symbolize waves which surround Prince Edward Island.The school is currently the largest in the
province in terms of student enrollment and 2nd largest in terms of area. The high school offers both French and English language and
the International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme.

My experience in CRHS

Not going to lie if you want to come here to study you will feel disappointed.
The rural is a nice place to meet some new people or take some non-academic ctivities, but for academic no.
You shall know CRHS is a relative smaller public school compare to others in larger provinces, so both the budget and resources are
